# About

Toronto practitioner, working across classical Chinese medicine and several spiritual traditions. Practicing publicly since 2008, in clinical practice since 2015, and in public ritual work since 2016. Teaching qigong since 2013, teaching TCM and acupuncture since 2018.

One practitioner, three doors. Each door has its own intake, its own standards, its own scope. Accountability to the pattern in front of us, not the surface story, holds across all three.

---

## Clinical practice

Registered Acupuncturist (R.Ac) in Ontario. Formal TCM training through the Ontario College of Traditional Chinese Medicine. Continuing study beyond the core curriculum in classical Chinese medicine: the historical clinical traditions passed down through family-transmitted practice, the medical lineages that served the imperial courts, and advanced needling technique studied with the late Professor Jin Zhang, a UNESCO-recognized representative of the Traditional Chinese Acupuncture Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

The approach is classical Chinese medicine, grounded in the traditional pattern-differentiation method and informed by the older transmission lineages that sit behind the modern TCM curriculum.

## Ritual and divination

Mao Shan Shangqing (茅山上清) and folk Daoist lineage holder, received from Robert Youngs at his passing in 2020. Youngs received the lineage from Jerry Alan Johnson. Mao Shan Shangqing is a foundational Daoist ritual tradition, rooted in the Mao mountains and the Shangqing school, with classical lineage going back to the fourth century.

Additional ritual training in Chinese Esoteric Buddhist (唐密) and Wu (巫) lineages through the Results Over Image Institute under Andrieh Vitimus, where I now teach as a trainer.

Ritual work is spiritual work, not healthcare. Sits outside R.Ac scope, not a substitute for medical, mental health, legal, or other professional care.

## Service to the profession

Former Director of Marketing on the board of Traditional Chinese Medicine Ontario (TCMO), the provincial professional association for Registered Acupuncturists and Traditional Chinese Medicine Practitioners.

In 2022, helped lead marketing and outreach for the cross-sector coalition that successfully opposed Schedule 5 of Bill 88 (Working for Workers Act, 2022), legislation that would have repealed the Traditional Chinese Medicine Act and dissolved the profession's regulatory college. Coordinated 14 letters of support from national regulatory bodies, industry organizations, and suppliers, and participated in newspaper and radio interviews including CBC News. The amendment removing Schedule 5 passed in March 2022, preserving the regulatory framework for Traditional Chinese Medicine in Ontario.
